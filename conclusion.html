Conclusion

The research on the anticancer potential of medicinal plants in oral cancer therapy reveals promising results. Medicinal plants such as Neem (Azadirachta indica), Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum), Jamun (Syzygium cumini), Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis), and Ginger (Zingiber officinale) contain potent phytochemicals that exhibit significant anticancer properties.

These plants contain compounds like flavonoids, alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that have been shown to inhibit cancer cell growth, induce apoptosis (programmed cell death), reduce inflammation, and prevent tumor development. The antioxidant properties of these plants also help in protecting healthy cells from damage caused by free radicals.

Traditional Ayurvedic knowledge, combined with modern scientific research, provides a holistic approach to oral cancer management. These natural therapies offer potential alternatives or complementary treatments to modern cancer therapies, with the advantage of fewer side effects and improved quality of life for patients.

While further research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms of action and to develop standardized formulations, the current findings strongly support the potential of medicinal plants in oral cancer treatment. Integrating these traditional therapies into mainstream healthcare could significantly improve patient outcomes and provide more treatment options.
