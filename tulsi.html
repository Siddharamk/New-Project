03

About the Plant

Tulsi is a highly valued medicinal and aromatic herb widely used in traditional medicine, especially in Ayurveda. It is a small perennial shrub that grows about 30–60 cm in height. The plant has green or purple leaves, a strong aromatic smell, and small purple or white flowers.

Tulsi is known for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties, making it one of the most important medicinal plants in traditional Indian pharmacology.

04

Historical Background

Tulsi has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for more than 3000 years. In Indian culture, it is considered a sacred plant and is often grown in courtyards and temples. Ancient Ayurvedic texts describe Tulsi as "The Queen of Herbs" due to its wide range of medicinal benefits.

It has traditionally been used for treating respiratory disorders, infections, stress, and inflammatory diseases, making it a cornerstone of both spiritual and therapeutic practice across the Indian subcontinent.

05

Origin and Distribution

Tulsi is believed to have originated in India and Southeast Asia. Today it is widely cultivated in India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and other tropical and subtropical regions of the world. The plant grows well in warm climates with moderate rainfall and is commonly found in gardens and near homes across India.

06

Plant Parts Used for Oral Cancer

The main parts of the Tulsi plant used in oral cancer research and treatment include:

  • Leaves — most commonly used; rich in eugenol, rosmarinic acid, and flavonoids
  • Essential oil from leaves — high concentration of active anticancer compounds
  • Stems — contain ursolic acid with documented anti-proliferative activity
07

Anticancer Mechanism

Antioxidant Activity
Neutralises free radicals that can damage DNA and lead to cancerous mutations in oral cells.
Apoptosis Induction
Triggers programmed cell death in cancer cells, preventing uncontrolled tumour growth.
Anti-inflammatory
Reduces chronic inflammation associated with cancer development in oral mucosal tissues.
Tumour Inhibition
Eugenol and ursolic acid suppress proliferation of oral cancer cell lines in vitro.
Immunomodulation
Enhances the body's immune response against tumour cells and pathogenic agents.
08

Traditional Uses

  • Treatment of cough, cold, and respiratory infections
  • Relief from fever and inflammation
  • Improving general immunity and vitality
  • Treatment of skin diseases and wound healing
  • Used as a stress reliever and adaptogen
  • Used in herbal teas and Ayurvedic preparations for general health and wellness

Tulsi remains one of the most important medicinal plants in Ayurveda, valued for both spiritual significance and therapeutic benefits.
