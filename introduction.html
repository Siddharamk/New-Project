Introduction

Oral cancer is one of the most common forms of cancer globally, accounting for a significant portion of cancer-related deaths. It affects various parts of the oral cavity, including the lips, tongue, cheeks, floor of the mouth, hard and soft palate, sinuses, and pharynx. The prevalence of oral cancer is increasing due to factors such as tobacco use, alcohol consumption, poor oral hygiene, and human papillomavirus (HPV) infection.

Conventional Treatments

Traditional treatment methods for oral cancer include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and targeted therapy. While these approaches can be effective, they often come with severe side effects such as pain, disfigurement, difficulty swallowing, and suppression of the immune system. This has led researchers to explore alternative and complementary therapies, including the use of medicinal plants.

Medicinal Plants in Oral Cancer Therapy

Medicinal plants have been used for centuries in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and herbalism to treat various ailments, including cancer. Recent scientific research has identified several bioactive compounds in medicinal plants that exhibit anticancer properties. These compounds work through various mechanisms, such as:

  • Inhibiting cancer cell growth and proliferation
  • Inducing apoptosis (programmed cell death) in cancer cells
  • Preventing angiogenesis (formation of new blood vessels that feed tumors)
  • Reducing inflammation in the affected area
  • Enhancing the body's immune system to fight cancer
  • Protecting healthy cells from the toxic effects of chemotherapy and radiation
Key Benefits of Medicinal Plants:

Medicinal plants offer several advantages over conventional treatments, including fewer side effects, lower cost, and accessibility. They can be used as complementary therapies to enhance the effectiveness of existing treatments and improve the quality of life for cancer patients.

Scope of the Project

This project aims to explore the anticancer potential of specific medicinal plants in oral cancer therapy. We will examine the scientific evidence supporting their use, understand their mode of action, and discuss their application in clinical settings. By highlighting the role of medicinal plants, we hope to contribute to the development of novel and effective treatment strategies for oral cancer.

Therefore, medicinal plants are considered a promising natural therapy for oral cancer management, offering a holistic approach to treatment that addresses both the disease and the well-being of the patient.
